White Blood Cells (Leukocytes)

What are Leukocytes

There are many different types and all contain a big nucleus.

Phagocytosis:

Phagocytosis is a process where in a cell binds to the item it wants to engulf on the cell surface and draws the item inward while engulfing around it. The process of phagocytosis often happens when the cell is trying to destroy something, like a virus or an infected cell, and is often used by immune system cells.

Some WBCs fight diseases by making antibodies to destroy invaders by dissolving them. Others make antitoxins to break down poisons.
