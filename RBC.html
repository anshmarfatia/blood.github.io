Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes)

What are Erythrocytes

RBCs are made in the bone marrow of some bones like ribs, vertebrae and some limb bones.

They have a shape of a biconcave disc that is round and flat without a nucleus and can change shape to an amazing extent, without breaking, as it squeezes single file through the capillaries.

They contain haemoglobin, a molecule specially designed to hold oxygen and carry it to cells that need it. haemoglobin which combines with oxygen in areas of high concentration to form oxyhaemoglobin

Oxygen + Haemoglobin ------> Oxyhaemoglobin


Did You Know:

Blood has the colour red beacause blood consists of mostly Red Blood Cells/Erythrocytes that give it the colour red.
